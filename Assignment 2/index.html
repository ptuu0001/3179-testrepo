What Makes A Country Liveable?

What makes a country a first world country? What makes a country a second world country? These are some questions I wondered while sipping on my morning coffee. So, I decided, why not write an article on this topic. There are many factors that determines a country’s liveability. While this article cannot account for all of them but can at least show the main contributing factors. According to the World Health Organisation (WHO), there are factors known as the determinants of health that influence an individual’s health (1). These determinants of health include but are not limited to social, economic situation, physical living environment and individual’s behaviour. This article will explore various factors that can affect individual’s livelihood and see how they compare against other countries.

The World Happiness Report is an annual survey that collates various quality of life factors, to calculate and rank countries based on a happiness score (2). As the name suggests, a higher score would mean a country/its citizens are more happy. While the rest of the graphs and visualisations in this article may not directly contribute in the happiness scoring process, they are definitely still factors that influences an individual’s happiness level.
